Open Research Call

October 15, 2020

Hello, fellow witches.

Some of you listening in may know me from our school days together, but, if
my suspicions about how the pair of devices that my dearest grandmother
gifted me is correct, then most of you do not.

So, introductions are in order. My name is Aster the Witch. And yes, I know.
The Witch. It’s been, what? Three years since I’ve graduated? And still, I’m just the Witch.
All of my covenmates from school have all earned their titles already. I’ve been getting letters
signed The Sea Witch, and The Swamp Hag. It’s quite frankly embarrassing!

Well, my fellow witches, that is going to change!

Even those of you who have never met me will recognize the title that has been in my family for generations.
A title that I intend to take.

My great-great-grandmother was the original Creature Witch. Her and every generation after her has written
and re-written the fundamentals on how we understand the magical creatures that surround us.

And that’s where I’ve come to a little bit of a... problem. My ancestors didn’t exactly have the foresight to leave any
readily undiscovered creatures.

. So, fellow witches, if you are hearing this message, I implore you to send me any and everything you suspect
might be evidence of a creature in your area. Unidentified hairs, paw prints, strange markings, or trails, objects
moving on their own. At the very least, I’ll be able to send you an excerpt from the family collection to identify the
creature and aid you in your problem, but if we’re lucky I’ll have a mystery on my hands.

Happy hunting!
